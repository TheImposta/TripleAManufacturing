About Triple A

How plastic bags are formed

Our films are produced using blown-film and cast-film extrusion processes. Thermoplastic resins are melted and forced through a die to form a thin film. For blown-film, air is used to expand the tube of molten polymer which is then cooled and flattened to form a continuous sheet. For cast-film, the molten polymer is extruded onto a chill roll and cooled. The sheet is then slit, sealed, and converted into bags with precise cutting and sealing machinery.
